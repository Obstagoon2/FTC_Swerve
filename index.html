Exploring Coaxial Swerve Drives in FTC

A coaxial swerve drive is an advanced drivetrain where each wheel module can rotate independently (steer) while also being powered for translation. In a coaxial design, the steering and drive power are transmitted via concentric shafts, allowing for a compact and often robust module. This explorer provides a curated list of notable coaxial swerve drive examples from the FTC community, complete with links to their design files, code, and videos.

How to Use This Explorer

Use the navigation panel on the left to select a team or project. The main area will update to show detailed information and links for that specific swerve drive implementation. This tool is designed to help teams research, learn from, and build upon the work of others in the community.

Team 19376 "Robo-Rams"

A compact and effective 3D-printed coaxial swerve module from the CENTERSTAGE season.

Overview

The Robo-Rams developed a highly compact coaxial swerve module that heavily utilizes 3D-printed parts for accessibility. The design is notable for its integrated dead-wheel odometry and clean packaging, making it a strong reference for teams looking to build a competitive and reproducible swerve drive.

Mechanical Details

  • Utilizes bevel gears for power transmission.
  • Integrates REV Through Bore Encoder.
  • Designed for Gobilda pattern.
  • Mainly 3D Printed (PETG/Onyx).

Key Features

3D-Printable Compact Integrated Odometry

David Cao's "Baby Swerve"

An extremely small and lightweight coaxial swerve module design.

Overview

This design by David Cao is famous in the FTC community for its incredible miniaturization. "Baby Swerve" pushes the limits of what is possible with COTS parts and 3D printing, resulting in a module that is exceptionally light and compact. While potentially less robust than larger designs, it serves as an excellent design study in efficiency and minimalist engineering.

Mechanical Details

  • Ultra-lightweight and compact form factor.
  • Uses a planetary gearset for steering.
  • Heavily reliant on precise 3D printing.
  • Designed around micro motors.

Key Features

Miniature Lightweight Community Classic

Team 21553 "Checkmate"

A robust, metal-based coaxial swerve from the POWERPLAY season.

Overview

Team Checkmate's swerve is a great example of a durable, high-performance module built primarily from COTS metal parts. This design prioritizes reliability and stiffness, making it suitable for high-impact gameplay. It's an excellent resource for teams who prefer machining and metal construction over 3D printing.

Mechanical Details

  • Primarily constructed from Gobilda parts.
  • Uses a large bearing for rotation.
  • Focus on rigidity and durability.
  • Exposed motor design.

FTCLib (Software Framework)

The essential software library for controlling advanced drivetrains in FTC.

Overview

While not a physical mechanism, FTCLib is a critical resource for any team implementing a swerve drive. It provides a robust, pre-built software framework for handling the complex mathematics (kinematics and odometry) required to control a swerve drive. Using FTCLib saves teams hundreds of hours of software development and provides a reliable foundation to build upon.

Key Software Components

  • SwerveModule class for individual module control.
  • SwerveDrive class for chassis kinematics.
  • PID controllers for steering motors.
  • Support for field-centric control.
